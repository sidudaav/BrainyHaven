What Is Dementia?

Dementia is not a specific disease. It is a term that describes symptoms of memory loss and thinking ability. The greatest risk factors for dementia are age, genetics and smoking/alcohol use. Unfortunately, there is no cure for this devastating illness yet; however, there are many ways one can reduce their risk for dementia and even improve their memory. Dementia happens when there are drastic, negative changes to the brain cells. This can severely affect the ability for the cells to transmit signals and communicate with each other, causing a change in behavior and a decline in reasoning skills and cognitive ability. Alzheimer’s disease, for example, is a common type of dementia where the patient loses the ability to remember recent events.

The Brain

The brain is an organ found in every human that controls the central nervous system. It is arguably the most important and most complex part of the body. We wouldn’t be able to pick up a cup of coffee or read the local newspaper in the morning without our brains. The brain is made up of nine parts, each with their own functions and specialties; however, these parts work together all the time to make sure their human runs well. The nine parts are shown below.

Occipital Lobe

Responsible for vision and contains the visual cortex.

Temporal Lobe

Integrates memories with the five senses.

Parietal Lobe

Processes movement, temperature, taste and touch.

Fontal Lobe

Controls voluntary movement (walking, eating, kicking).

Thalamus

Relays information to other parts of the brain.

Hypothalamus

Maintains and grows body through hormones.

Pituitary Gland

Controls other glands (thyroid, adrenal) and releases hormones.

Cerebellum

Coordinates movement including balance, speech and looking around.

Spinal Cord

The center of sending signals from the brain to around the body.
